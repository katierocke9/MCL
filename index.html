LEARNING PHILOSOPHY AND PRACTICES


Ages 4-11
Small Group Learning


GOAL: For your child to be intrinsically motivated to learn and investigate! Get excited to learn :)


The learning lessons and practices throughout the learning year will be eclectic. We will be drawing from several different theories and practices since all children learn and develop differently. We will use parts of Reggio Emilia’s and Maria Montessori’s practices. The project approach used by the Reggio philosophy allows children to investigate and dig deeper while simultaneously developing relationships. Children learn through and with art given the opportunity to explore, discover and problem solve. Art will be an important aspect to learn with and through.

Therefore, children are taught actual life skills they will develop and use for the rest of their lives. The Reggio approach uses many of Piaget's and Vygotsky’s theories as well. Jean Piaget’s cognitive development theory fits into the project approach; in addition, Lev Vygotsky’s zone of proximal development also is part of the project approach, which includes the guidance of an adult.

Furthermore, Maria Montessori also believes in an environment that is stimulating, age-appropriate, and inviting to children. Her hands-on environment and self-correcting materials also show great respect for the competence of children. We believe that with loving guidance, the right amount of stimulation, and teacher assistance, all children can unfold and develop their full potential.

Establishing a routine and utilizing the classroom as a 3rd learning couch is essential. Building a community among the children and learning coaches will establish an environment that is open for growth, exploration, and trust.
